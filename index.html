Yellowstone National Park

Yellowstone National Park was established in 1872 by Ulysses S. Grant. It was the first national park in the world!

Hiking

There are a wide variety of hiking trails throughout Yellowstone. Day hikes will travel through awesome scenery and can take you near wildlife. There are also many overnight hikes, though you'll need to check with a ranger about campsites.

Fishing

Many of the streams and rivers in Yellowstone have many fish, as does Yellowstone Lake. Permits are run by the Park, and you'll need to get one before you fish. You'll only be able to fish for particular species, depending on the needs of the Park and on the permits.

Wildlife

"I was almost eaten by a bear!!!"

Bob Jones

There is a wide variety of wildlife throughout the Park, including bears, wolves, bison, elk, and many others. The natural ecosystem is relatively pristine, as the land has been park for so long. You'll likely see many different animals, though you should always be careful around larger animals!

Sightseeing

The area has a lot of geothermal activity, with hot rock and water coming up from beneath the land. There are many interesting geological features, including the Yellowstone Canyon, hot springs, and many geysers. Old Faithful is perhaps the most famous, erupting regularly for many years.
